Introduction

The Logging Vocabulary defines key terms and concepts used in managing and analyzing system logs. Understanding this vocabulary helps standardize communication between development, security, and operations teams, facilitating the correct interpretation and use of log data.

What's in it?

    Definitions of common terms such as event, log entry, log level (INFO, WARN, ERROR), timestamp, correlation, audit trail, etc.

    Explanations of different log categories (e.g., application, system, security).

    Concepts related to log data integrity, confidentiality, and availability.

    The difference between synchronous and asynchronous logging, and their impact on performance and security.

How to use it

    Apply consistent terminology in documentation and logging tools.

    Facilitate training and communication between technical teams.

    Use the vocabulary as a basis for defining logging policies and procedures.
